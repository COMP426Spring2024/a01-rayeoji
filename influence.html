INFLUENCE & CONNECTIONS

Several popular mythological figures have similar attributes to Mami Wata, regardless of cultural or geographic origin. Though it is often unclear whether Mami Wata influenced them, or the other way around, the similarities between these figures is undeniable. And the fact that her legends exist all over the world might make you question how "mythical" she actually is.

Figure Culture/Region Similarities
Yemoja Yoruba religion; West Africa & Latin America Both are powerful female water deities who are worshipped widely for their maternal and supernatural traits.
Mermaids International folklore: Europe, Africa, the Americas, Asia and the Middle East Like Mami Wata, mermaids are female half human, half fish creatures that dwell in the seas of the world.
Sirens Ancient Greek and Roman mythology Mami Wata is often grouped with the sirens as being dangerous female sea creatures who use their sexual lure to draw men to their deaths in the sea.
Nymphs Greek folk religion Both are female water/nature spirits that can be depicted as hypersexual with the ability to drive men mad with desire.
Succubi Medieval and Biblical folklore; Jewish origins Succubi are female demons whose goal is to seduce men and women into sexual acts, whether while awake or in their dreams, and drain their life force through sex. Like Mami Wata, they are representations of sexual temptation and are able to bewitch their victims with their eroticism.
